Observations


Claim 1: The earth isn't actually getting warmer. Summer is hot and winter is cold. Temperature changes are seasonal and nothing more.

Analysis:

  • The global average temperature anomaly displays a steady increasing trend beginning around 1980.

  • The global average temperature anomaly in January of 1980 and 2018 (end of the dataset) is 0.26 °C and 0.85 C, respectively.

  • Therefore, the global average temperature anomaly increased by 0.0155 °C per year over that span of 38 years.

  • However, the temperature anomaly over the past 18 years (2000 to 2018) has been increasing at a rate of 0.0256 °C per year, indicating that the global average temperature anomaly is increasing at a non-linear rate.
